The "Greyhound" Maneuver

Tactical breakdown of the 270° intercept.

1. The Screen

Greyhound is traveling in front of the convoy, screening for U-boats. A U-boat is detected dead ahead, attempting to outrun them.

2. U-Boat Evades

The U-boat initiates a hard left turn, aiming to loop back toward the vulnerable convoy.

3. The 270° Right Turn

Instead of chasing the U-boat into its turn (which would leave Greyhound trailing due to a larger turning radius and high momentum), Hanks orders a massive 265° sweeping turn to the right.

4. The Intercept Angle

This radical turn achieves a crucial tactical advantage: it completely changes the approach angle. Greyhound is now bearing down on the U-boat perpendicular to its path.

5. The Kill Zone

From Greyhound's perspective, the U-boat is crossing left-to-right across their bow. Because of Greyhound's closing speed, the U-boat cannot evade. Greyhound drives directly over the top, dropping depth charges exactly across the submarine's trajectory.
